DAY 1

1. What is a LLM?

Neural networks designed to understand, generate and respond to human like texts.
These are deep neural networks trained on massive amounts of text data.

2. Large ? Language models? 

Large since these models have billions of parameters.

Language models since these models do a wide range of NLP tasks, question answering, translations,
sentiment analysis and much more.

3. How are these different from earlier NLP models

LLMs can do wide range of NLP tasks and earlier NLP models were
designed for specific tasks like language translations etc.


DAY 2 

1. Why finetune after pre train?

To make our model task specific

Pretraining is a unsuvpervised learning and its auto regression because after a word
is predicted it is taken into the training loop i guess and the next word is predicted
and it goes on.

Finetuning is mostly done on labelled data.


Steps for building a LLM : 

1. Train on a large corpus of raw text data.
Raw text means here regular text without any labelling information

2. First training stage of LLM is also called Pretraining to create a foundational model

3. After obtaining the pretrained LLM, we can further train LLM on labelled data.

4. There are two types of popular finetuning 
    a. Instruction finetuning- 
        Labelled dataset consists of Instruction answer pairs.

    b. Classification Tasks-
        Labelled datet consist of text and associated labels


5. Transformers - 

Transformers are the backbone of most state-of-the-art LLMs because
they support parallel processing, advanced context awareness, and highly 
scalable training, all thanks to self-attention mechanisms.

PS : attention is all you need is a beautiful paper.

Steps -
1. Input text sent
2. Preprocessing Steps
    a. Tokenization - Breaking down words and sentences and assigning ids

3. Encoder 
    a. Vector embedding is implemented on Preproccessed texts 
    This is very important to ensure semantics hold even after assigning numerical ids.
    Each token is a number, but numbers dont hold grammar semantics or meaning so we convert
    them into vectors embeddings.

4. Embeddings generated in multi dimensional manner.

5. Decoder 
    a. The vector embeddings are fed to decoder
       During use Decoder only gets Encoder output and previously generated tokens(what is has
       said so far)
       From these it predicts the next word.

Note : One word predicted at a time.

Attention mechanism - Allows us to model the dependencies between words without regards to how close
apart or how far apart the words are.

Self attention- Is an attention mechanism relating different positions of a single sequence in order to
compute a representation of the sequence.
It means it alows model to weigh importance od different words/tokens relative to each other.
It helps us capture long range dependenciesso that the model can look both far behind and closer to identify
the next word.
