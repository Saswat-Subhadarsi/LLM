DAY 1

1. What is a LLM?

Neural networks designed to understand, generate and respond to human like texts.
These are deep neural networks trained on massive amounts of text data.

2. Large ? Language models? 

Large since these models have billions of parameters.

Language models since these models do a wide range of NLP tasks, question answering, translations,
sentiment analysis and much more.

3. How are these different from earlier NLP models

LLMs can do wide range of NLP tasks and earlier NLP models were
designed for specific tasks like language translations etc.

1. Why finetune after pre train?

To make our model task specific

Pretraining is a unsuvpervised learning and its auto regression because after a word
is predicted it is taken into the training loop i guess and the next word is predicted
and it goes on.

Finetuning is mostly done on labelled data.


Steps for building a LLM : 

1. Train on a large corpus of raw text data.
Raw text means here regular text without any labelling information

2. First training stage of LLM is also called Pretraining to create a foundational model

3. After obtaining the pretrained LLM, we can further train LLM on labelled data.

4. There are two types of popular finetuning 
    a. Instruction finetuning- 
        Labelled dataset consists of Instruction answer pairs.

    b. Classification Tasks-
        Labelled datet consist of text and associated labels
